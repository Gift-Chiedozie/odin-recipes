Ingredients:

  • 4 plum tomatoes or 1 tin of plum tomatoes
  • 4 red bell peppers
  • 3 scotch bonnet pepper, or according to preference
  • 1 ½ of large red onion
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tbsp of fresh ginger
  • 1 cup of chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp of curry
  • 1 tbsp of thyme
  • Seasoning cube, as needed
  • 1 pack of spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp of butter or olive oil
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables
  • 3 tbsp of tomato paste

Directions:

  1. For the stew base (pepper mix), you can either:

    Blend your plum tomatoes, red bell peppers, 1 large onion, 3 scotch bonnet peppers, garlic and ginger. Then, boil the blended mixture on medium - high heat till the water is reduced and has thickened significantly to look like a paste; this should take about 20 minutes if placed on high heat.

    OR

    Cut your tomatoes, peppers, onions, garlic and ginger. Season with a little olive oil and salt. Place in a pan and roast in a preheated oven of 350C for 20 minutes. Blend after

  2. Add water to the pot, bring to a boil on low - medium heat. Add salt and a little oil.
  3. Add a pack of spaghetti and allow to cook for about 8 - 10 minutes. Drain out the water from the spaghetti using a colander (sieve). Set aside.
  4. Heat up oil in a pan and sauté onions till it’s translucent.
  5. Add your pepper mix (as described above), tomato paste and chicken stock. Mix well and allow to fry for about 5 minutes.
  6. Taste for seasoning and adjust with seasoning cube(s) if needed. Add curry and thyme.
  7. Add spaghetti into the mixture and mix well for the sauce to get into the spaghetti. Fry (stirring occasionally) for about 3 - 5 minutes. And it’s done! Enjoy
